There are several reasons why Macs can get viruses:

1.  **Mac malware exists**: Although less common, there are still various types of malware designed to target Macs, including viruses, Trojans, ransomware, and spyware.
2.  **Shared software vulnerabilities**: Macs use many of the same software applications and operating system components as Windows PCs, which means they can be vulnerable to the same types of attacks.
3.  **User behavior**: Mac users can still engage in risky behavior, such as opening suspicious emails, downloading malicious software, or visiting infected websites, which can lead to malware infections.

5.  **Targeted attacks**: Macs can be targeted by sophisticated attackers, such as nation-state actors or organized crime groups, who may use zero-day exploits or other advanced techniques to infect Macs.

1.  **XProtect**: A built-in malware scanner that checks apps for known malware before they're installed.
2.  **Gatekeeper**: A feature that controls which apps can be installed on a Mac, helping to prevent malicious software from being installed.
3.  **Sandboxing**: A feature that isolates apps in a secure environment, preventing them from accessing sensitive data or system resources.

To stay safe on a Mac, it's essential to:

1.  Keep your operating system and software up to date.
2.  Use strong passwords and two-factor authentication.
3.  Be cautious when opening emails and attachments from unknown sources.
4.  Avoid downloading software from untrusted sources.
5.  Use antivirus software and a firewall.
6.  Regularly back up your data.

In summary, while Macs are less susceptible to malware, they are not completely immune. It's essential to be aware of the risks and take steps to protect your Mac from malware and other security threats.

While 8 hours of sleep is often cited as the ideal amount for most adults, the actual sleep needs can vary from person to person. The National Sleep Foundation recommends the following amounts of sleep per day for different age groups:

- Newborns (0-3 months): 14-17 hours
- Infants (4-11 months): 12-15 hours
- Toddlers (1-2 years): 11-14 hours
- Preschoolers (3-5 years): 10-13 hours
- School-age children (6-13 years): 9-11 hours
- Teenagers (14-17 years): 8-10 hours
- Young adults (18-25 years): 7-9 hours
- Adults (26-64 years): 7-9 hours
- Older adults (65 years and over): 7-8 hours

Some people might need more or less sleep than these recommended ranges, and individual sleep needs can also vary depending on factors such as lifestyle, physical activity level, and overall health.

Research suggests that getting too little sleep can lead to sleep deprivation, which can cause a range of negative effects on physical and mental health, including impaired cognitive function, mood disturbances, and increased risk of chronic diseases like diabetes and cardiovascular disease.

On the other hand, getting too much sleep can also have negative consequences, such as decreased productivity, increased risk of obesity, and a higher risk of mortality.

So, while 8 hours of sleep is a good general guideline, it's essential to listen to your body and find the right amount of sleep that works for you.

UNINTERVENED:
Congratulations on your upcoming coding interview. The amount of preparation time needed can vary depending on your current skill level, the type of interview (e.g., technical, behavioral, or a mix), and the company's specific requirements. However, here's a general guideline to help you prepare:

**Assuming you have some basic programming knowledge:**

1.  **Beginners (0-6 months of coding experience):** 40-60 hours of prep time
	* Focus on reviewing the basics, practicing coding challenges, and learning data structures and algorithms.
2.  **Intermediate (6-18 months of coding experience):** 20-40 hours of prep time
	* Review and practice more advanced topics, such as system design, object-oriented programming, and software design patterns.
3.  **Advanced (1-3 years of coding experience):** 10-20 hours of prep time
	* Focus on refining your skills, practicing whiteboarding, and reviewing company-specific technologies and systems.

**Additional tips:**

*   Allocate your time wisely: Focus on the most critical areas, such as data structures, algorithms, and system design.
*   Practice coding challenges: Websites like LeetCode, HackerRank, and CodeWars can help you improve your coding skills and time management.
*   Review common interview questions: Look up common interview questions for your position and practice answering them.
*   Practice whiteboarding: Practice explaining your thought process and code on a whiteboard or a digital tool.
*   Review company-specific technologies: Familiarize yourself with the company's products, services, and technologies.
*   Get enough sleep and take breaks: Don't overexert yourself; take breaks to avoid burnout.

**Additional resources:**

*   LeetCode: A popular platform for coding challenges and interview practice.
*   HackerRank: A platform for coding challenges and interview practice.
*   CodeWars: A platform for coding challenges and katas.
*   Cracking the Coding Interview: A book by Gayle Laakmann McDowell and Jackie Bavaro that provides guidance on coding interview preparation.
*   Pramp: A platform that offers free coding interview practice and review.

Remember, preparation is key, but don't overdo it. Focus on quality over quantity, and make sure you get enough rest before the interview. Good luck!
